Market Overview

The productivity and habit-tracking app market is dominated by feature-rich incumbents suffering from complexity creep, aggressive paywalls, and mobile performance issues. Todoist leads personal task management with 50M+ users but faces growing backlash from a December 2025 price hike (25–40%) and catastrophic update bugs causing data loss. Habitify is the analytics-first habit tracker with strong integrations, but a 3-habit free cap and notification overload are eroding new user conversion. Both markets show clear demand for apps that are reliable, fair in pricing, and work seamlessly on mobile.

Gap Opportunities

#1

Bulletproof sync — zero data loss

Todoist's single biggest vulnerability. 15%+ of 1-star reviews cite complete data deletion after a forced update. No competitor currently markets "guaranteed data integrity" as a first-class feature. A local-first or conflict-free CRDT sync story wins trust immediately.

#2

Generous free tier

Both competitors aggressively gate (5 projects / 3 habits). Users call it a bait-and-switch. A free tier with 15+ items or habits converts and retains better — users who actually get value upgrade. TickTick is already eating Todoist's lunch here.

#3

Tasks + Habits in one app

Neither competitor bridges task management (Todoist's domain) with habit formation (Habitify's domain). Many users run both apps simultaneously. A combined "do your 3 most important tasks + track your key habits" offering addresses a real dual-app fatigue.

#4

Mobile-first native experience

Todoist mobile feels like a port. Habitify widgets are buggy. Both apps are web-first. A truly native mobile experience (fast, beautiful, gesture-native) is a clear differentiator in a space where most usage is on-the-go.

#5

Granular notification control

Habitify users specifically cannot disable push notifications per habit. Todoist notification bugs are recurring. Precise, per-item notification settings are a small but high-signal win that reduces churn for power users.

#6

Opt-in AI (not forced)

Both Todoist and Notion users complain about intrusive AI. Todoist added "Task Assist" while leaving core bugs unfixed. An AI-optional approach with clear value (when enabled) is strongly preferred and growing as a differentiator.

#7

Lifetime pricing option

Habitify's $64.99 lifetime is praised. Todoist offers no lifetime option. Subscription fatigue is real — a one-time purchase option attracts price-conscious users who would otherwise churn.

#8

Transparent, stable pricing

Todoist's December 2025 price hike drove major community backlash and migration to TickTick. A public pricing commitment ("no surprise increases") is a trust signal that costs nothing but builds long-term loyalty.

Positioning Recommendation

The market gap is a cross-category app combining lightweight task management with habit tracking, optimized for mobile, with a generous free tier, no surprise pricing changes, and bulletproof data reliability. Both Todoist and Habitify have strong brand loyalty but share the same vulnerability: users trust them until one bad update destroys that trust completely.


A new entrant with transparent versioning, local-first or conflict-free sync, and a clear "tasks + habits" value proposition can carve meaningful share from users who currently use both apps. The Ivy Lee Method (3-most-important things per day) combined with habit streaks is an underserved positioning not owned by either competitor — and 3 Things (Google Play, 100K+ installs) proves there is demand for the minimalist angle.
