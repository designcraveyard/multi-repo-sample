## Market Overview

The productivity and habit-tracking app market is dominated by feature-rich incumbents suffering from complexity creep, aggressive paywalls, and mobile performance issues. Todoist leads personal task management with 50M+ users but faces growing backlash from a December 2025 price hike (25–40%) and catastrophic update bugs causing data loss. Habitify is the analytics-first habit tracker with strong integrations, but a 3-habit free cap and notification overload are eroding new user conversion. Both markets show clear demand for apps that are reliable, fair in pricing, and work seamlessly on mobile.

## Gap Opportunities

Ranked by user demand signal from review and web analysis:

1. **Reliable sync without data loss** — Todoist's single biggest vulnerability. Users demand bulletproof sync. 15%+ of 1-star reviews mention data loss. Any new entrant with a rock-solid sync story wins trust immediately.

2. **Generous free tier** — Both competitors gatekeep heavily (5 projects / 3 habits). Users repeatedly cite this as the reason they switch. A free tier with meaningful utility (10+ items) converts better.

3. **Mobile-first experience** — Todoist mobile feels like a desktop port. Habitify's widgets are buggy. A natively-fast mobile experience is a clear differentiator.

4. **Task + habit in one app** — Neither competitor bridges task management (Todoist) with habit formation (Habitify). Users use both apps simultaneously. A combined offering addresses this directly.

5. **Notification control** — Habitify users specifically complain about inability to disable push notifications per habit. Granular notification settings are a small but high-signal win.

6. **No forced AI** — Both Todoist (Task Assist) and Notion users complain about intrusive AI features they didn't ask for. An opt-in AI approach is strongly preferred.

7. **Lifetime pricing option** — Habitify's $64.99 lifetime is praised. Todoist offers no lifetime option. Subscription fatigue is real; a one-time purchase option differentiates.

8. **Transparent pricing** — Todoist's Dec 2025 price hike caused major backlash. Users want predictable, honest pricing. No surprise increases.

## Positioning Recommendation

The market gap is a **cross-category app combining lightweight task management with habit tracking**, optimized for mobile, with a generous free tier, no surprise pricing changes, and bulletproof data reliability. Both Todoist and Habitify have strong brand loyalty but share the same vulnerability: users trust them until one bad update destroys that trust. A new entrant with transparent versioning, local-first or conflict-free sync, and a clear "tasks + habits" value proposition can carve meaningful share from users who currently use both apps. The Ivy Lee Method (3-most-important things) combined with habit streaks is an underserved positioning not covered by either competitor.
